Legal Framework & Consumer Rights

⚖️ Know Your Rights & Laws

📌 Consumer Rights in India

  • 🛡️Right to Safety: Protection from hazardous goods and services.
  • ℹ️Right to be Informed: Accurate details about quality, price, and standards.
  • 🛍️Right to Choose: Freedom to select goods/services fairly.
  • 📢Right to be Heard: Voice concerns before authorities.
  • ⚖️Right to Seek Redressal: Compensation for unfair practices or defects.
  • 🎓Right to Education: Awareness of rights and remedies.

📖 Consumer Protection Act, 2019

  • 📌 Section 2(6): Defines “Complaint.”
  • 📌 Section 2(47): Defines “Unfair Trade Practices.”
  • 📌 Sections 17 & 34: Establishment of Consumer Commissions (District, State, National).
  • 📌 Allows online filing of complaints → foundation of ODR.

💻 Information Technology Act, 2000

  • 🔐 Section 43A: Compensation for failure to protect personal data.
  • 👤 Section 66C: Punishment for identity theft.
  • 🔏 Section 72: Breach of confidentiality and privacy.
  • ✅ Recognizes electronic records & digital signatures.

🤝 Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996

  • 📝 Section 7: Defines arbitration agreements (including digital).
  • 🤝 Section 30: Encourages settlement via mediation/conciliation.
  • ⚖️ Arbitral awards are legally enforceable → strengthens ODR settlements.

🛒 Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020

  • 📌 Section 4: Duties of e-commerce entities to protect consumers.
  • 📌 Section 5: Mandatory online grievance redressal mechanisms.
  • 📌 Platforms like Amazon/Flipkart must provide ODR-based systems.

🌍 Global Framework

  • 🌐 UNCITRAL promotes cross-border online dispute resolution.
  • 🇪🇺 EU ODR Regulation (2016): European platform for e-commerce disputes.
